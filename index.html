Our Donation Plan

Shelters

Our Trust started out as a day care centre for street children. Over a period of time, we realised that a safe space is the most critical need for any child to realise his/her own full potential. Thus, we started residential programmes to provide safe, comfortable and friendly shelters for children rescued from the street or any other distress situations.

Education

The intensive engagement with street children to transform them into capable and thinking individuals is carried out by enabling access to education and empowering children with skills. We help channelize the energy and intelligence of young children into different forms of education and give children a safe environment to study and hone their skills.

Nutritious Meals

Nutritionally-balanced, hot-cooked meals are served at all residential centres and contact points. The meals are prepared under the supervision of staff members to ensure safe and hygienic practices. Unwell children with different nutritional requirements are provided special meals including protein supplements, additional fruits and milk as prescribed by the doctor.

Flood Relief Program

Around 13 lakh people were affected in Sylhet city, including five Upazilas. Surma and the Kushiyara rivers started flowing above the danger level. The heavy torrential rainfall in the north-eastern region and India's Meghalaya and Assam has left parts of the district paralysed since May 12. The cyclonic storm Asani worsened the situation. Some families have taken shelter in higher grounds, but many more live in waist-high water, with no place to go. 13 lakh people are water-bound. As a part of the emergency response, our volunteer team is working on the spot and planning to provide emergency support, food, water purifying tablets, medicine, and more to the people in flood-affected areas. It will be beneficial if you lend a hand.
